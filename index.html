About Me

My name is Ye Fon Ho. I was born in Manhattan, New York, but I grew up in a small town in North Jersey called Pompton Lakes. I have two sisters, one older and one younger. The older one attends Jefferson University in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, majoring in fashion design.

Growing up, music was a big part of my life. I sang tenor in the choirs and played most of the brass instruments. I started with the trumpet in elementary school and throughout high school, I played the baritone, the euphonium, the trombone, the tuba, and the trumpet for different ensembles. For senior year, I was also the brass captian for the marching band. Aside from music, taekwondo was also a big part of my life. I started when I was seven years old and continued for almost ten years. I taught as well as took classes. I reached the rank of second degree black belt and junior instructor before I left. My greatest achievement in taekwondo was winning a gold medal at the 2014 ATU National Taekwondo Championships.

My sophomore year, I also joined the tennis team and played through my senior year. I did not put that much time into the sport, so it was more of a hobby to me. Other hobbies of mine include playing League of Legends with my friends and watching Netflix (or just television in general). My favorite ongoing shows on Netflix are BoJack Horseman, Orange is The New Black, and Unbreakable Kimmy Schmidt.

I did very well in high school. I finished in the top ten of my class and got high honor roll almost every semester. I was not very sure of what I wanted to do in my future, but I knew it was something computer related. Living in a small town, the high school did not offer any computer-related courses, so I would be going into college with no prior computing knowledge. I decided to choose computer science as my major and RIT as my school, but, unfortunately, my success in high school did not translate very well into college.

My first year at RIT was not a very pleasant experience. I did not do well in many of my first semester classes and ended up having to withdraw from my computer science course. Second semester, I decided to change the focus of my courses to accounting. My grades definitely were better that semester, but I also found out that I most certainly did not enjoy accounting. After a talk with my academic advisor, I decided to shift back into computing with the web and mobile major.

On the more positive side, I did enjoy being a part of the RIT Pep Band and the RIT Singers during my first year. In the pep band, I played the trombone, and in the choir, I sang the tenor part. I also met some people and joined their team for some on campus League of Legends tournaments. I intend to be a part of the same groups this year.

This will be my second year at RIT. I know that I cannot keep bouncing between majors, so I will have to stay with Web and Mobile Computing. So far, I am enjoying this course very much, so hopefully, I will be able to stick with this major and eventually graduate.
